Jim Thorpe

An American Sports Ledgend

Here's a time line of Jim Thopre's life accomplishments:

  • May 28, 1888: Jim Thorpe is born as Wa-Tho-Huk (Bright Path) in a cabin near Prague, Indian Territory (now Oklahoma).
  • Early 1900s: Thorpe attends the Carlisle Indian Industrial School in Pennsylvania, where he excels in athletics under the guidance of coach Pop Warner.
  • 1912: Thorpe competes in the 1912 Stockholm Olympics, winning gold medals in both the pentathlon and the decathlon. His achievements lead King Gustav V of Sweden to famously declare, "You, sir, are the greatest athlete in the world."
  • 1913: Thorpe plays professional baseball for the New York Giants, Cincinnati Reds, and other teams in the Major Leagues.
  • 1915: Thorpe joins the Canton Bulldogs, one of the early teams of the American Professional Football Association (later renamed the NFL). He becomes one of the most famous football players of his time.
  • 1920: Thorpe serves as the first president of the American Professional Football Association, which later becomes the National Football League (NFL).
  • 1920s-1930s: Thorpe continues to play professional football for various teams, including the Canton Bulldogs, Cleveland Indians, and Chicago Cardinals.
  • 1950: Jim Thorpe is named the "Greatest Athlete of the First Half of the Century" by the Associated Press.
  • 1951: Thorpe passes away on March 28, 1953, in Lomita, California.
  • 1982: After years of advocacy by Thorpe's family and supporters, the International Olympic Committee restores Thorpe's amateur status and recognizes him as the sole gold medalist of the 1912 Olympics.
  • 1986: The Jim Thorpe Foundation is established to promote his legacy and support Native American athletes.
  • 2001: The Associated Press names Thorpe the "Male Athlete of the Century."
